Periodic Table Trends

Initial Exposure

Before this class all I knew about the periodic table was that there are different families like noble gasses or akali metals. When I first learned about periodic trends I didn't understand what a trend was referencing and I certainly didn't know concepts like forces of attraction and repulsion, atomic radius, electron affinity, ionization energy and electronegativity.

My Road to Mastery

As I worked through the lessons I learned about the different patterns that occur across the elements on the periodic table. I also learned and mastered concepts like electron shielding and knew why it affects atomic radius.

I also learned two other trends: electron affinity and ionization energy. Ionization energy is the amount of energy required to remove an electron from an atom. I know that because elements on the right side are closer to a full octet, it is harder to take an electron away. Electron affinity is the energy released when an electron is added. Since the elements on the right really want to complete the full octet, when they get the electron/electrons needed they give off more energy. To master these concepts I would pick a random elements and list them from least to greatest in each category.
