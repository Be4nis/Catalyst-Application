History of the atom

Although this unit wasn't as "fun" as the other ones, I found it interesting that the discovery of protons, neutrons and electrons happened only around 150 years ago. Before this lesson I thought the model of an atom has been known since ancient Romans ruled Europe.

The Experiments

Intially when I learned about the cathode ray experiment, I didn't understand it. Overtime I understood that a cathode-ray tube is a glass vacuum tube with metal plates on either side connected to an electrical circuit. When the circuit is turned on, a beam of light shoots from the cathode. Thomson used this device to find the negatively charged electrons. The experiment that discovered the nuclues was the gold foil experiment. For me, this experiment was easier to understand. Rutherford and his colleagues shot a ray of alpha particles from a lead box into a gold foil. 99.99% of the particles went through the foil but the other particles were deflected away from the nucleus which disproved Thomson's plum pudding model.
